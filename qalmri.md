

Question
- Broad: 
	- What is the mechanistic basis for "infant amnesia"?
- Specific: 
	- Are infants able to encode unseen information?
	- Is infant amnesia caused by encoding or retrieval failures/incapabilities?

Alternative
- Encoding failure: development of the hippocampus is long and slow, reaching maturity during adolescence.
- Retrieval failure: rodent studies show evidence of "memory engrams" forming during infancy. In human infants, there are also observable behavioral differences when interacting with familiar vs. unfamiliar environments
 
Logic
- 

Method
- fMRI of awake infants while performing a subsequent memory task

Results
- Evidence of encoding, suggests failure of retrieval


Interpretation
